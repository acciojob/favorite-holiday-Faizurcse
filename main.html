Kashmir


Kashmir is a region located in the northern part of the Indian subcontinent, largely in the northern part of the Indian-administered union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. The region is known for its stunning landscapes, including mountains, valleys, and lakes.

  1. Srinagar: The summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir, Srinagar is famous for its beautiful Dal Lake, houseboats, and Mughal gardens like Shalimar Bagh and Nishat Bagh.
  2. Gulmarg: Known for its picturesque meadows and being a popular skiing destination in winter, Gulmarg also boasts the Gulmarg Biosphere Reserve and the Gulmarg Gondola, one of the highest cable cars in the world.
  3. Pahalgam: A charming town surrounded by lush greenery and snow-capped mountains, Pahalgam is a base for the annual Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage and offers activities like trekking and golf.
  4. Sonamarg: Translating to "Meadow of Gold," Sonamarg is known for its pristine landscapes, glaciers, and trekking trails. The Sindh River that meanders through the valley adds to its scenic charm.
  5. Shalimar Bagh: One of the Mughal Gardens in Srinagar, Shalimar Bagh is a beautifully landscaped garden with terraced levels, flowing water, and fountains.
  • Rogan Josh: A flavorful and aromatic curry made with tender pieces of meat (usually lamb or goat), cooked in a rich and spicy red gravy.
  • Wazwan: Wazwan is a traditional multi-course meal in Kashmiri cuisine, often served during weddings and special occasions. It includes a variety of dishes such as kebabs, curries, and rice preparations like biryani.
  • Yakhni: A yogurt-based curry typically made with chicken or mutton, seasoned with aromatic spices like fennel and cardamom.
  • Haak Saag: Collard greens or spinach cooked with mustard oil and spices, a common vegetable side dish in Kashmiri cuisine.
  • Phirni: A creamy rice pudding flavored with saffron and cardamom, often enjoyed as a dessert.
